How To Use Google Meet for Online Video Meetings

By Bogdan Bele Last Updated on May 13, 2020

All in all, Google Meet is a very solid video conferencing product and a serious competitor for the likes of Microsoft Teams or Zoom.

 Share Tweet Share Share Email Comments

Google Meet, an easy to use free video conferencing tool, can now be used by everyone. It’s not just for the business sector anymore. Here’s how you can use it.

Google Meet Video Conferencing

Google Meet was initially only part of G Suite, about which you can find out more here . Meet is a simple and effective video conferencing tool and it’s both a web-based service and an app.

Google has decided to make Google Meet available to everyone, on the simple condition that they have a Google Account. The only caveat is that private users have their conferences restricted to 60 minutes. However, this limit had been waived temporarily at the time this article was written, until September 30, 2020 .

As you will soon see, using the service is extremely easy and it can turn into a great way of keeping in touch with your loved ones.

Using Google Meet

Start by going to this page and clicking Start a meeting .

The service will ask you to allow access to your camera and microphone. After providing it, click Join Now . If you want to share your screen directly, you can also click  Present. 

After that, you will be provided with joining info for your meeting. You can either send the link to your contacts and they will be able to join via the web or mobile app or Add people  by Google account.

You can also set up a meeting straight from Google Calendar. When you schedule an event there, the option for Google Meet will be shown there as well.

During the meeting itself, clicking the screen will bring up a toolbar. This allows you to switch your microphone or camera off, but also to start presenting immediately. It also gives you instant access to the meeting details, should you need to add more people to your meeting. That’s also where adding files to the meeting is possible.

Speaking of presenting, you can click  Present now  if you need to emphasize your point by sharing your screen, a window, or a specific tab in Chrome.

The  Turn on captions  button is also useful. You can add live captions and the app will transcribe what participants are saying and it’s quite accurate. In case you weren’t aware, YouTube can also do something similar and you can personalize the fonts , too.

All in all, Google Meet is a very solid video conferencing product and a serious competitor for the likes of Microsoft Teams or Zoom . The fact that it’s free can only make it even more tempting.
